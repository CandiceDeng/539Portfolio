I graduated from Univeristy of Washington.

My name is Nan, a first year UMSI student. I graduated from the University of Washignton in 2015, major in Public Administration (MPA). In UW, I have primarily worked on improving my communication and managerial skill as a leader in non-profits.

In a non-profit organization, we frequently need to deal with multi-missions with limited financial and human resources. A successfully operated human-computer collaboration mode could fundamentally decrease our unnecessary time spending on information classification and give us more chances to discover other populations those are waiting for our help. After a whole week’s self-study of MySQL and Eclipse, I independently designed a data correspondence system, which enabled us to correspond donors’ aspirations with the recipients’ basic information more accurately through establishing a data dictionary. It has increased our working efficiency on materials distribution to a great extent. This experience makes me aware that depending merely on either management or technical skills is not enough to facilitate real changes in non-profit organizations, and eventually inspires me to seek for opportunities to combine these two capacities. Even after finishing the internship, I still could not stop thinking our currently unsolved problem about how to create a system to collect the feedbacks from our targeted populations in order to evaluate our work and make future amendment and improvement.
